Resilience from within

Part 1

Stress resilience
Contains
  • Trichoderma harzianum
A consortium of advanced strains

Part 1 is a living consortium of advanced Trichoderma harzianum strains, symbiotic fungi that partner with the plant itself. Working from within, they raise the crop's tolerance to stress so it keeps performing when conditions turn against it.

  • Built for stress. The fungi help the crop push through drought, heat and salinity so growth holds steady when it matters most.
  • A consortium, not a single strain. A blend of advanced strains covers far more conditions than any one microbe can, which is what sets it apart from off-the-shelf, single-strain products.
  • Sets up the season. More resilient, better-supported plants lay the groundwork for the program's typical 8 to 10% yield gain, with greater gains under stress.

Nutrition from the soil

Part 2

Nutrient-use efficiency
Contains
  • Bacillus licheniformis
  • B. megaterium
  • B. pumilus
  • B. subtilis
  • B. amyloliquefaciens
Five-strain consortium
~1×10⁸ CFU/g each

Part 2 is a five-strain Bacillus consortium, beneficial bacteria that unlock nutrients already present in your soil and help the plant use them more efficiently. The result is strong growth on less fertilizer.

  • Unlocks your soil. Five complementary Bacillus strains free up nutrients and minerals that are present but out of reach, putting them back to work for the crop.
  • 30 to 50% less nitrogen. Better nutrient-use efficiency supports a real cut in nitrogen inputs, and the fertilizer bill that comes with them.
  • Compounds with Part 1. A more resilient crop makes fuller use of what Part 2 unlocks, which is why the two are always run together as one program.
